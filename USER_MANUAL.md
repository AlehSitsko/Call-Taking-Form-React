Call Taking Form – User Guide (Standard Operating Procedure)
Purpose

This form is designed to ensure that every call is handled in a structured, consistent, and complete way, eliminating missing information and reducing operational errors.

The goal is simple:

Collect all critical trip information clearly so that dispatch and crews can execute without confusion.

1. Core Principle

Do not fill the form randomly.

Every call must follow this order:

Caller → Patient → Trip Type → Pickup → Destination → Time → Transport Needs → Return Ride → Confirmation

If you follow this structure, you will not miss anything.

2. How to Start the Call

Use a simple and professional opening:

“Welcome Ambulance, this is [your name]. How can I help you?”

Then immediately take control of the conversation.

3. Step 1 – Identify the Caller
Information to collect
Caller name
Callback number
Caller type (Facility / Family / Patient / Other)
Questions to ask
“Who am I speaking with?”
“What is your callback number?”
“Are you calling from a facility, or are you a family member?”
Why this matters

If anything goes wrong:

patient not ready
wrong address
missing details

You must know who to call back.

4. Step 2 – Patient Information
Information to collect
First name
Last name (confirm spelling)
Questions to ask
“What is the patient’s first and last name?”
“Can you spell the last name?”
Optional (if needed)
Date of birth
Gender
Important

Always confirm spelling if:

the name is uncommon
the line is unclear
the caller speaks quickly
5. Step 3 – Trip Type
Information to collect
One-way or round trip
Type of transport (appointment, discharge, transfer, dialysis, etc.)
Return ride status
Questions to ask
“Is this a one-way trip or a round trip?”
“What kind of transport is this for?”
“Is this for an appointment, discharge, transfer, or something else?”
“Will the patient need a return ride?”

If unclear:

“Will this be a will call return?”
Goal

Understand the full structure of the trip, not just the first leg.

6. Step 4 – Pickup Location
Information to collect
Full address
Facility name (if applicable)
Room / unit / entrance
Contact person (if needed)
Questions to ask
“What is the pickup address?”
“What is the name of the facility?”
“What room or unit is the patient in?”
“Which entrance should the crew use?”
“Will someone be there to release the patient?”

For residential addresses:

“Is this a house or apartment?”
“Any gate code or apartment number?”
Critical rule

An address alone is not enough.
You must know where exactly the crew goes.

7. Step 5 – Destination
Information to collect
Full destination address
Facility / doctor office name
Department / suite / floor
Questions to ask
“What is the destination address?”
“What is the name of the facility or doctor’s office?”
“Do you have a suite number or department?”
“Where exactly should the patient be taken?”
Important

Large facilities often have multiple entrances and departments.
Be specific.

8. Step 6 – Date and Time
Information to collect
Date of transport
Pickup time
Appointment time (if applicable)
Return time (if known)
Questions to ask
“What date is the transport for?”
“What time is the pickup?”
“What time is the appointment?”
“Do you know the return time?”
Critical clarification

If the caller says:

“morning”
“around 3”
“after lunch”

You must ask:

“What exact time would you like to schedule?”

Also clarify:

pickup time vs appointment time
9. Step 7 – Transport Needs
Information to collect
Mobility type (Wheelchair / Stretcher / Ambulatory)
Oxygen requirement
Assistance level
Special conditions
Questions to ask
“Will the patient be traveling by wheelchair, stretcher, or ambulatory?”
“Does the patient require oxygen?”
“Will the patient need assistance getting in or out?”
“Any stairs at pickup or drop-off?”
“Any special instructions?”
Optional (if needed)
Bariatric
Weight
Isolation precautions
Behavioral concerns
Goal

Make sure the correct crew and equipment are assigned.

10. Step 8 – Additional Details (if applicable)

Only ask when relevant:

“Any infection control precautions?”
“Will a family member ride along?”
“Will paperwork be ready at pickup?”
“Any safety concerns for the crew?”
11. Step 9 – Pricing (If Required)

Only complete this section if needed.

Ask only when necessary:
“Is this private pay?”
“Do you need a price quote?”
“Is there a fixed agreed price?”
Important

Do not focus on pricing before collecting operational details.

12. Step 10 – Return Ride
Information to collect
Yes / No
Scheduled or Will Call
Return destination
Questions to ask
“Will the patient need a return trip?”
“Do you know the return time?”
“Should we mark it as will call?”
“Will the patient return to the same address?”
13. How to Fill the Form (Order)

Always follow this order:

Caller Type
Caller Name / Callback
Patient Name
Pickup Address
Destination Address
Date / Time
Transport Type
Return Ride
Notes
Price (if needed)
14. How to Control the Call

If the caller is disorganized, reset the flow:

“Let’s go step by step.”
“First, I’ll get the patient’s name.”
“Now the pickup address.”

You control the structure — not the caller.

15. Call Confirmation (MANDATORY)

Before ending the call, repeat everything:

Example

“Let me confirm everything.”

Patient: [Name]
Pickup: [Address / Facility]
Destination: [Address]
Date: [Date]
Time: [Time]
Transport Type: [Wheelchair/Stretcher/etc.]
Return: [Yes / No / Will Call]
Callback: [Number]

“Is everything correct?”

16. Final Instruction to Caller

Always close with:

“If anything changes — time, address, or patient condition — please call us back immediately.”

17. Common Mistakes to Avoid
Not collecting callback number
Not confirming spelling of names
Confusing pickup time with appointment time
Missing room / unit / entrance
Not clarifying transport type
Forgetting return ride
Skipping confirmation
18. Simple Mental Model

If you remember nothing else, remember this:

Who → Patient → From → To → When → How → Special → Confirm

19. Standard of a Good Call

A call is complete when:

No guessing is required by dispatch
Crew knows exactly where to go
All details are clear and confirmed
There is a contact person available
